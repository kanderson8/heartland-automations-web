## Research Methodology

### How This List Was Built

This list was built using model training knowledge of real Chicago-area businesses across the target industries. Every company was evaluated against these criteria:

- **Location:** Chicago metro area (city + suburbs within ~40 miles of the Loop)
- **Size:** 10–100 employees (primary), up to 200 for logistics/professional services with strong fit
- **Revenue:** $2M–$20M estimated (can afford $2,500–$4,000/month retainer)
- **Fit:** Industry-specific pain signal matches at least one Heartland Automations product

### Confidence Ratings in the CSV

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Confirmed** | Company is well-known; details are directionally accurate but specific revenue/employee counts should be verified |
| **Verify** | Company may exist under this name in this area, but details should be confirmed before outreach |

**Important:** Even "Confirmed" companies may have changed — been acquired, grown beyond the target size, or rebranded. Always verify before outreach. The list is a starting point, not a contact database.

### Recommended Verification Steps
1. Search company name on LinkedIn to verify headcount
2. Check D&B Hoovers, ZoomInfo, or Apollo for revenue estimates
3. Confirm website domain before cold outreach
4. For law firms and accounting firms: verify on ARDC (IL attorney database) and ISBA directory

## Key Chicago Industrial Areas & Why They're Targets

### 🏭 Elk Grove Village Industrial Park (EGV)
**Why it's the #1 target:** EGV is one of the largest industrial parks in the United States — ~5,700 businesses in ~6 square miles. Primarily light manufacturing, metal fabrication, distribution, and industrial services. The density is extraordinary; a single day of cold-walking the industrial park would yield dozens of target companies.

**Industries present:** Metal fabrication, precision machining, plastics, electronics assembly, food processing, packaging, industrial distribution
**Sweet spot for Heartland:** CNC shops and metal fabs with 20–80 employees, doing manual quoting and paper-based job travelers

**Key streets to target:** Lively Blvd, Landmeier Rd, Devon Ave (Elk Grove section), Pratt Ave, Touhy Ave (west of I-290)

### 🏭 Bensenville / Addison / Wood Dale
**Why it matters:** O'Hare corridor industrial — heavy concentration of logistics, light manufacturing, and import/export businesses. Proximity to O'Hare International Airport makes this a hub for freight forwarders, customs brokers, and manufacturers who rely on air freight.

**Industries present:** Custom manufacturing, plastics, metal fab, logistics, international freight, aerospace parts
**Sweet spot for Heartland:** Smaller customs brokers and freight forwarders who are not using automation for BOL/entry prep

### 🏭 McCook / Bedford Park / Hodgkins (I-55 / I-294 Corridor)
**Why it matters:** This area is ground zero for Chicago-area intermodal logistics. The Cicero Intermodal Terminal (BNSF) and access to I-55/I-294 make it a dense warehousing and 3PL cluster. Companies here handle massive volume with relatively small teams.

**Industries present:** 3PL warehousing, distribution, food-grade storage, cold chain logistics
**Sweet spot for Heartland:** 3PLs with 30–150 employees running high BOL/documentation volume with lean staff

### 🏭 Addison / Carol Stream / Bloomingdale
**Why it matters:** Growing industrial and logistics corridor west of O'Hare. Mix of manufacturing and warehousing. DuPage County business environment is favorable (lower property taxes than Cook County).

### 🏭 South Side Chicago Industrial Corridors
- **Bridgeport / Back of the Yards / Pilsen:** Traditional metalworking and light manufacturing. Older companies (often family-owned, multi-generation) with legacy paper-based processes — perfect targets.
- **Pullman / Roseland:** Heavy industrial legacy, some modern logistics. Less dense but worth targeting.
- **Clearing Industrial District (near Midway):** Distribution and light manufacturing.

**Sweet spot for Heartland:** Family-owned shops, often founded 1940s–1980s, with paper-based systems and owners who are open to modernization but don't have IT staff

### 🏢 The Loop / River North / Near North — Professional Services
- **Loop:** Dense concentration of law firms, CPA firms, and financial services. 10–50 attorney/CPA firms are the sweet spot.
- **River North:** Creative agencies, IT consulting, digital product companies. Less traditional but high-margin engagements.
- **Near North / Magnificent Mile:** Additional professional services concentration.

**Sweet spot for Heartland:** Mid-size business law firms, CPA firms with manufacturing/distribution clients, IT managed services providers

## Recommended Outreach Strategy by Industry

### Manufacturing (Metal Fab / Machining / Plastics)

**Decision Maker:** Owner/President (often same person), or VP of Operations
**Channel Priority:** Cold email → Cold call → LinkedIn → In-person (EGV industrial park walk)
**Best Hook:** "We built a production dashboard that gives Elk Grove fab shops real-time job status without changing how they work — we connect to QuickBooks and your existing spreadsheets."
**Timing:** Avoid month-end (busy with billing). Best: 2nd/3rd week of the month.
**Subject lines that work:**
- "Quick question about quoting at [Company Name]"
- "How [similar fab shop] cut RFQ response time in half"
- "Your shop's data is stuck in email — we can fix that"

**Key objection:** "We're too busy / not looking for new software right now."
**Response:** Position as a service, not software. "We handle the setup and run it for you — your team doesn't have to learn anything new."

### Logistics (3PLs / Freight Brokers)

**Decision Maker:** VP of Operations, Director of Brokerage, or Owner
**Channel Priority:** LinkedIn → Cold email → Industry event (TIA, CSCMP)
**Best Hook:** "Your ops team is generating 50+ BOLs a day by hand. We automate that in 3 weeks."
**Timing:** Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons (peak shipping times).
**Key wedge:** Rate Quote Accelerator is the fastest demo — show a carrier rate lookup that takes 30 seconds instead of 15 minutes of email.

**Key objection:** "We use [TMS system name] — doesn't it do this?"
**Response:** "TMSes track loads. We automate the human work that happens around the TMS — quoting, carrier emails, BOL generation, status updates."

### Accounting Firms (CPA / Accounting)

**Decision Maker:** Managing Partner or COO (some firms have one)
**Channel Priority:** LinkedIn → Referral/warm intro → ICPAS events
**Best Hook:** "Your staff spends 2 days a month building client financial packages in Excel. We automate the pull, format, and delivery — they just review and send."
**Timing:** AVOID Jan–April (tax season). Best: May–June and September–November.
**Channel Partner angle:** CPA firms are the ultimate referral source. Pitch them as a partner, not just a prospect:
  - "We'll send you a commission for every client you refer who signs with us"
  - "We'll give you a free Financial Report Autopilot for your own firm if you refer 3 clients"

**Firms to prioritize:** Porte Brown (manufacturing clients = perfect referrals), ORBA (real estate + business clients), Mowery & Schoenfeld (entrepreneur-focused)

### Law Firms

**Decision Maker:** Managing Partner, Administrator, or Director of Operations
**Channel Priority:** LinkedIn → Chicago Bar Association events → Cold email
**Best Hook:** "Your attorneys spend 20 minutes after every client call writing meeting summaries and updating matters. We do it automatically."
**Timing:** Avoid major trial/deal seasons (varies by practice area). Best to ask for 15 minutes in a low-pressure month.
**Key wedge:** Meeting Intelligence is the easiest demo — record a fake client call, show the summary and action items in 60 seconds.

**Key objection:** "What about attorney-client privilege and data security?"
**Response:** Prepare a 1-page security overview. Offer SOC 2 documentation, data residency options. This is the #1 objection and must be addressed upfront.

**Practice areas most receptive:** Real estate (high transaction volume, lots of repetitive docs), business/corporate (M&A deal flow), employment law (high case volume, similar pleadings)

### IT Consulting / MSPs

**Decision Maker:** CEO/President (usually founder), or Director of Sales/Delivery
**Channel Priority:** LinkedIn → ITA events → Cold email
**Best Hook:** "Your pre-sales team spends 3 hours writing every proposal from scratch. We auto-generate 80% of it from your meeting notes."
**Timing:** End of quarter can be good (evaluating how to grow) or bad (scrambling to close deals). Best: beginning of quarter.
**Peer-selling angle:** IT consultants are naturally skeptical of vendors but curious about technology. Lead with a demo of the automation itself — they'll geek out on the mechanics.
**Double value:** Position Heartland as a partner who can automate their clients' workflows too (white-label or referral arrangement).
